# RemainFrame website direction research

Observed July 17, 2026. This document records the research behind the second interactive concept batch. It is not customer-facing copy.

## Product and audience constraint

RemainFrame is for nontechnical owner-operators: single-person businesses, realtors, creators, consultants, local service providers, trades, shops, wellness practices, hospitality businesses, and similarly small owner-led niches. It will not pursue enterprise clients.

The intended customer range is roughly 1–30 people, and no single vertical is the product. Real estate is one possible configuration among many, not the default story. RemainFrame is a powerful customizable agent that can be tailored by Dade Studio or further tailored by the customer. Depending on the business, it may act as a secretary, screen and pre-qualify prospects, identify stronger leads, support operations, organize projects, work with budgets and expenses, help an HOA board carry out its duties, or learn another specialized role. Website concepts must demonstrate this breadth without turning the product into a collection of fixed personas, model profiles, or industry packages.

The product should therefore feel sophisticated without asking a visitor to understand AI infrastructure. RemainFrame may support a deep overhaul behind the scenes, but the local buyer's front door is usually one narrow, valuable need. The service promise is that a tailored AI system can retain a useful understanding of their business, while RemainFrame handles setup and upkeep with them and expands only when it is useful.

## What the direct category does now

- [Mem0](https://mem0.ai/) proves its product with SDK setup, code, benchmarks, and infrastructure language. That is credible for developers and wrong for RemainFrame's buyer.
- [Letta](https://www.letta.com/) frames the problem as stateful agents and continuous learning. The category distinction is useful, but the research-lab and API posture is not.
- [Zep](https://www.getzep.com/) builds a rigorous visual world around context graphs, operational tables, governance, and scale. Its coherence is worth learning from; its enterprise posture is explicitly out of scope.
- [Supermemory](https://supermemory.ai/) makes memory concrete through inspectable product surfaces and also offers a personal product. Its breadth across APIs, filesystems, retrieval, plugins, and deployment makes the story too technical and diffuse for this audience.
- [Pieces](https://pieces.app/) leads with a human outcome: a searchable memory of work that happens across the day. Its concrete timeline, familiar sources, and privacy controls are closer to the buyer's mental model.
- [Personal AI](https://www.personal.ai/products) makes memory visible and editable, which supports trust. Its digital-identity language can become abstract or uncanny for an owner who simply wants better help with their business.
- [Kin](https://kin.newstandard.ai/) makes import and portability tangible. The useful translation is “we bring the useful parts of your business with you,” not “personal AI data center.”
- [Sintra](https://sintra.ai/industries/for-business) speaks plainly to solo founders and small-business owners and foregrounds fast setup. Its cast of AI employees, large overhaul promise, promotional pricing, repeated CTAs, and volume of claims create exactly the high-pressure, generic AI-funnel feeling RemainFrame should avoid.

## What buyer-fit leaders teach

- [HoneyBook](https://www.honeybook.com/) names familiar jobs first: keeping clients and projects on track, drafting personalized email, preparing project recaps, and providing human support. AI is presented as a helper inside recognizable work rather than as the product's main spectacle.
- [Square](https://squareup.com/us/en) starts from owner identity and aspiration, then lets visitors recognize their business type. Its “local legend” language and niche navigation show how a broad product can still feel made for a very small business.
- [Squarespace](https://www.squarespace.com/) translates a complex platform into specific owner categories such as real estate, creative services, wellness, home services, and local experiences. It repeatedly emphasizes ease, support, and professional outcomes instead of technical machinery.
- [Shopify](https://www.shopify.com/) sells ambition at the owner's scale, moving from solopreneur to larger outcomes without requiring the visitor to understand the platform beneath it. RemainFrame should borrow the confidence, not the scale narrative.

## Local-market reality

The [Pagosa Springs active member directory](https://member.pagosachamber.com/activememberdirectory) foregrounds owner-scale categories such as business and professional services, construction and contractors, outdoor recreation, property management, real estate, and wellness. The broader [Pagosa business directory](https://pagosa.com/business-directory/) also emphasizes lodging, vacation rentals, shops, local services, construction, hot springs, and recreation. Those are better scenario anchors than generic “teams” or enterprise departments.

Useful first wedges include a realtor's client follow-up, a property manager's guest or owner communication, a contractor's estimate and job history, a guide or hospitality operator's seasonal questions, a wellness provider's repeat-client preferences, and a creator's approved direction and voice.

## Design principles for RemainFrame

1. **Lead with relief.** The first promise is “stop starting over,” not memory, models, context, agents, or infrastructure.
2. **Make the service human.** Say that RemainFrame learns how the business works, sets the system up, and keeps it useful. Do not make the buyer feel they are adopting or administering a platform.
3. **Demonstrate one familiar moment.** A realtor follow-up, a creator returning to an approved direction, or a service owner replying to a repeat customer is more persuasive than a feature diagram.
4. **Keep the business at the center.** Tools may change around the work, but the site should never turn tool switching into a model-selection interface.
5. **Use ordinary control language.** “See what it knows. Correct it. Remove it. Take a copy.”
6. **Be specific about niches.** Show recognizable owner-led situations without pretending one generic workflow fits every business.
7. **Let craft carry the technical sophistication.** Scroll choreography, spatial depth, responsive material, and kinetic typography should make continuity felt. They should not become an AI-energy orb, network diagram, or dashboard.
8. **Build trust without theater.** No invented customer logos, testimonials, usage figures, benchmark claims, shields, or compliance walls.
9. **Avoid an overhaul posture.** The buyer keeps the tools and working habits they like. RemainFrame improves continuity around them.
10. **Never adopt enterprise language or buyer patterns.** No sales-engineering posture, governance dashboards, scale claims, or procurement framing.
11. **Enter through one useful job.** Show a specific owner-scale need first, then reveal that the same enduring business understanding can support more work over time.

## Concept architecture

The current batch explores four different ways to make the promise tangible:

1. **Already Briefed:** a cinematic, scroll-driven day in the life of one owner. The business details survive from one moment to the next.
2. **Pick Up Where You Left Off:** a bright editorial direction with an interactive lens that reveals the business knowledge beneath an ordinary request.
3. **The Business Book:** a warm, tactile folio that stays present as projects, days, and situations change around it.
4. **In Register:** a high-contrast graphic direction that shows the same customizable agent taking on entirely different roles across customer intake, lead screening, board and budget work, projects, and other small-business operations.

Each concept must use its own interaction language. None may include a model/profile picker, developer quickstart, dashboard, enterprise proof wall, or infographic explaining AI architecture.
